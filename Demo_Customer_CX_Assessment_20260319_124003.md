# Executive Summary

The network assessment reveals a **critical health posture** with an overall score of **36.9/100**, classified as **Critical Risk**. The network faces severe threats from expiring hardware and software support, coupled with significant security vulnerabilities. 

## Hardware EoL Risks
Two hardware platforms are **past End-of-Security Vulnerability Support (EOSVS)**: the **Nexus 93180LC-EX** (2 devices) and **ASR1001-X** (5 devices). These devices lack security patches post-August 2025, exposing critical infrastructure to attacks. Urgent migration is required to the recommended **C8500L-8S4X** for ASR1001-X and **Nexus 9336C-FX2** for Nexus 93180LC-EX. 

## Software EoL Risks
The **NX-OS 9.3(9)** (19 devices) and **IOS 15.2(7)E7** (8 devices) are operating with **no security fixes** post-April 2025 and March 2024, respectively. These platforms are running **legacy software** with no vulnerability support. Immediate migration to **NX-OS 10.4(7)** and **IOS 15.2(7)E13** is critical to maintain network security.

## Security Posture
The network has **1538 PSIRT advisories** (5.6 per device), with **29 Critical** and **87 High-severity vulnerabilities** affecting active devices. Devices running **IOS-XE 16.x** (3 devices) and **NX-OS 9.3(9)** (19 devices) are particularly vulnerable, with no security fixes available. The top devices (e.g., SITE42P-CUBE-R2) have **Critical** vulnerabilities that remain unpatched due to EoL constraints.

## Conformance Gap
The network has **96 non-compliant devices** (38% of the fleet). The **C9300** (16 devices) and **C9410** (21 devices) series are running outdated software (e.g., **17.6.4**, **17.9.3**). These devices are **behind recommended versions** (17.12.6), increasing their exposure to vulnerabilities.

## Cross-Domain Risk
Devices like the **ASR1000 series** (12 devices) face a **triple threat**: past EoL, high Field Notice exposure (5 High-risk alerts), and running legacy software (16.12.4). These devices are **critical for network operations** but lack security support, making them high-priority for replacement.

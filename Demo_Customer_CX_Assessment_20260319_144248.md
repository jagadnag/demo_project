# Executive Summary

The network assessment reveals a **critical overall health posture** with an overall score of **36.9/100**, placing the network in the **Critical Risk** category. The primary drivers of this score are **software end-of-life (EoL)** issues and **security vulnerabilities**. While hardware EoL is well-managed (95.3/100), the software EoL score is **zero**, indicating no versions are within their recommended lifecycle. Security vulnerabilities are pervasive, with an average of **5.6 PSIRT advisories per device** and a domain score of **3.3/100**.

## Hardware EoL Analysis

The hardware EoL posture is **excellent**, with only **54 devices (20%)** affected by EoL risks. The top hardware EoL risk is the **Nexus 93180LC-EX** switch, with **2 devices** already **past EoL** (bulletin EOL13146) and **200 days remaining** until the last date of security support. The next risks are **ASR1001-X** (5 devices, 18 months remaining) and **ISR4431/K9** (6 devices, 1 year remaining), both with **medium risk** due to approaching end-of-sale dates.

## Software EoL Analysis

The software EoL posture is **extremely poor**, with an overall score of **0/100**. The most critical risk is **NX-OS 9.3(9)** on **19 devices** (bulletin EOL14694). This version reached its **end-of-vulnerability-support (EoVSS)** date of **April 10, 2025**, meaning it receives **no further security patches**. Customers should migrate to **NX-OS 10.4(7)**. Other critical risks include **IOS-XE 16.x** versions (3 devices) and **older NX-OS 10.3(6)** versions (2 devices). The recommended migration strategy for all affected versions is to upgrade to **IOS-XE 17.12.6** or **NX-OS 10.4(7)**.
